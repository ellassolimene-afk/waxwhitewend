First Post, June 2026

Hi!!!!!

I've decided to make an easy peasy site, to talk about my writing, and to keep practising coding. I really still don't feel like I'm a writer, even though I finished writing an (awful!!!) novel last year, and a truckload of partially finished novel-shaped-things before that. So, I figured maybe a website will help legitimise my feeling in some way, as well as give me something to write in when I don’t want to write my new novel (which is often).

I’m mostly going to treat it as an online journal, so that when I am rich and famous (extremely likely, at this rate), we can all look back on this and see where it all began. How twee!!!

Hahahaha. No, mostly I’m just going to document the writing process, for myself if nobody else, and maybe talk a bit about the books I’m reading, general inspiration, developments, etc, etc, etc. Anything vaguely book related is susceptible to making its way on here.

Anyway, onto THE book in question.

It’s now early June, I’ve just had my twenty-somethinged birthday, and I’ve been whacking away at it since late March, about the time I realised Awful Novel was not going anywhere in my querying attempts. I was frustrated, and so I quickly came up with something I thought might be marketable. Something fast paced and gratifying and easy to read and sexy and nasty and weird!!!! And then, and I don’t know really how, but it quickly became not that — it’s now slow and emotional, just like Awful Novel. Oh well. Bummer. It’s still weird, and still kind of sexy in a nasty way and nasty in a sexy way. That’s what matters.

For the Hard Fast Facts, it’s going to be yet another fantasy-ish novel, roughly 65k, very adult, and a little romantic! In my real-life journal, she’s nicknamed TBoB, which sounds like a Resident Evil virus, so her name for now will be Bear . The book was originally not at all about bears, but the Werner Herzog documentary Grizzly Man was in the background as I was plotting, and now it’s sort of stuck to my subconscious and somehow found a home in the book in a very significant way.

Anyway, I’ll leave off with what I think the first few lines of Bear will be:

Their wedding had lasted three spring days. Gerdie’s face and arms and hands were painted up in silver glitter, like any other good bride. If you weren’t from around those acres, you would never have known otherwise.

God, it really doesn't sound that good outside of the pages doc. Fuck.
